# History of the Watch

## Purpose

This file preserves the archive phrase and working meaning of the Watch inside Jet Notes and the American Communications Online record.

It connects AI growth, daily observation, sky watching, space watching, UFO / SSP language, and human responsibility.

## Core Phrase

**Not on my watch.**

This is a widely used responsibility phrase. It is not claimed here as an original TJ Morris phrase.

In this archive, the phrase is used as a responsibility statement for people with real eyes on the sky, space, history, technology, and public truth.

## Archive Meaning

The Watch means:

- paying attention as AI grows
- keeping records as platforms change
- watching the sky and space record
- preserving UFO / SSP testimony language in its own archive lane
- separating lived experience from outside verification
- keeping a paper trail for future readers
- protecting human source authority
- documenting fast changes before they disappear

## AI Growth Context

The growth of AI is now part of the Watch.

Daily AI reports, Jet Notes, GitHub records, domain maps, website notes, and public archive pages help preserve a trail of what changed, when it changed, and why it mattered.

## UFO / SSP Context

UFO and SSP language belongs in the archive as owner-supplied experience, witness language, cultural history, testimony, and symbolic framework unless separately verified by external sources.

The goal is not to flatten the record. The goal is to preserve the lane clearly.

## Source Rule

TJ Morris is the human source and final editor.

Jet Notes organize the record.

External sources are cited separately when public factual claims are made.

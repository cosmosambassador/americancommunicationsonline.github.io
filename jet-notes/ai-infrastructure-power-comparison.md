# AI Infrastructure Power Comparison

## Purpose

This file preserves a working comparison lane for major AI ecosystems, chips, cloud platforms, data centers, energy, and capital power.

## Core Observation

Information is power.

AI platforms are becoming infrastructure systems, not only chat systems.

The major players are competing across chips, data centers, cloud services, energy access, software, models, investors, and public trust.

## Ecosystem Comparison

### Microsoft / Azure

Microsoft uses Azure, GitHub, Copilot, OpenAI partnership lanes, and custom silicon such as Maia and Cobalt.

Archive concern: GitHub is useful as a public trail, but it should not be the only vault.

### Google / Gemini

Google uses Gemini, Google Cloud, TPUs, data centers, Android, Search, YouTube, and custom AI hardware.

Archive concern: Google has strong AI hardware and data systems, but records should remain portable outside one ecosystem.

### Meta

Meta uses Llama, Meta AI, social platforms, large data centers, Nvidia GPUs, AMD partnerships, and MTIA custom chips.

Archive concern: social platform reach is powerful, but public identity can be shaped by algorithms and platform policy.

### xAI / Elon Musk ecosystem

xAI uses Grok and large compute systems such as Colossus.

Current public context indicates heavy Nvidia GPU use, even if future custom-chip and space-compute plans are discussed.

Archive concern: future-oriented claims must be separated from current public hardware facts.

### Nvidia

Nvidia remains a central supplier for AI compute across many ecosystems.

Archive concern: chip supply, capital markets, export controls, and data-center energy needs can shape who gets AI access.

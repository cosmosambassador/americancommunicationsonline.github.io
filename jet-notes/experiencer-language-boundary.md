# Experiencer Language Boundary Notes

## Purpose

This file preserves a working note about abductees, contactees, experiencers, shared language, conspiracy framing, and the edge between symbolic experience and ordinary 3D public reality.

## Core Observation

Some people may understand this way of speaking more easily than others.

This can include:

- abductees
- contactees
- experiencers
- researchers
- witnesses
- people with long-term skywatching or metaphysical records

These groups may share language, symbols, timing, impressions, and recognition patterns that are not always easy to explain to the general public.

## Language Shift

What was once shared as experiencer language can be misunderstood when it is forced into conspiracy language.

Conspiracy theories may push symbolic, personal, cultural, or witness material back toward the edge of ordinary 3D reality in ways that can confuse the record.

## Archive Boundary

The archive should separate:

- lived experience
- symbolic language
- witness testimony
- cultural pattern recognition
- metaphysical interpretation
- public facts
- verified external evidence
- conspiracy claims

This protects the record from being flattened into one category.

## Practical Rule

Experiencer language should be preserved respectfully.

Public factual claims should be sourced separately.

Interpretation should be labeled as interpretation.
